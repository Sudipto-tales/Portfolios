Introduction To FigJam

Figma is an online design tool that allows people to create, edit, and collaborate on designs in real time. It's used for creating websites, apps, logos, and other visual content. Think of it as a digital whiteboard where designers can work together, no matter where they are, to build and share their projects.

  • Dylan Field and Evan Wallace began working on Figma in 2012.
  • In June 2022, Google for Education partnering with Figma to bring its design and prototyping platform, as well as FigJam

FigJam: FigJam files are online whiteboards packed with tools for exploration and discovery. People often use FigJam for collaborative activities like meetings and brainstorms, as well as async activities like diagramming, planning, and research.

Reasons Become

Popular

Collaborative Features for Workshops: FigJam is designed for activities like workshops, meetings(Realtime Collaboration), and brainstorming(sticky notes) sessions. With features like breakout rooms, facilitators can guide discussions, and everyone can collaborate or participate in separate groups for specific topics.

    Inserting Figma Frames: Users can directly import designs, wireframes, or prototypes from Figma into FigJam for group discussion, feedback, or review.

  • Cross-Tool Compatibility: FigJam and Figma files can be navigated easily, enabling smooth transitions between brainstorming and design execution.
